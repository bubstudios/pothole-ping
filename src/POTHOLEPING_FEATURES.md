# PotholePing — Feature List

---

## 🗺️ Live Pothole Map
See every reported pothole on an interactive map with color-coded markers — yellow for minor, orange for moderate, red for severe, dark red for dangerous. Tap any marker to see details, photos, and who's responsible for fixing it.

## 📋 List View + Search
Toggle to a list view to browse all reports. Search by address, city, or description to find what you're looking for.

---

## 🎤 Voice-Activated Reporting While Driving
The app listens hands-free. Just say **"Pothole Ping"** and it drops a pin at your exact GPS location. No typing, no tapping — perfect for when you're behind the wheel. Voice listening starts automatically if your phone already trusts the mic.

## 📍 Tap-to-Drop Manual Reporting
Prefer to report manually? Tap the Report button, then tap anywhere on the map to drop a pin. Add severity, description, and a photo.

---

## 🔍 Automatic Jurisdiction Lookup
Every report automatically figures out who's responsible for fixing that road — city, county, state, or federal. It finds their **phone number**, **email**, **website**, and even **Open311 API endpoint** if they have one. All verified in real time.

## 📧 One-Tap Contact the Authorities
Each pothole shows a jurisdiction card with one-tap Call, Email, and Website buttons. The email comes pre-filled with all the pothole details — just hit send.

## 🤖 Auto-Submit to Authorities
If the jurisdiction's email or Open311 endpoint is found, the report is automatically submitted to them the moment you create it. No extra steps.

---

## ✅ Community Verification (Upvotes)
See a pothole someone else reported? Confirm it's still there with one tap. Your vote carries more weight the more you contribute — heavy users have up to 2x influence.

## 🟢 Mark as Fixed
If you see a pothole has been repaired, mark it fixed. You earn reputation points and the community knows it's handled.

## ⚠️ Dispute a Fix
If someone marked a pothole fixed but it's still there, dispute it. It goes back to active status and the person who falsely claimed it loses reputation.

## ⭐ Reputation System
Every action earns karma — reporting, confirming, marking fixes. The more you contribute, the more your votes count. Leaderboard ranks everyone by karma.

---

## 🚨 Proximity Danger Alerts
While driving with the app open, it constantly monitors your GPS. If you're within 100 meters of any reported pothole, a pulsing red warning appears center-screen showing the severity and distance. Alerts auto-enable when voice listening is on.

## 📏 Nearest Pothole Distance
The proximity alert button always shows how far you are from the closest reported pothole.

---

## 🔥 Heatmap Overlay
Toggle a heatmap to see where potholes cluster. Filter by severity (all/minor/moderate/severe/dangerous) and time range (this week, this month, last 3 months, all time). Shows a count of active hotspots.

## 🛑 Duplicate Detection
If you try to report a pothole within 100 feet of an existing unfixed one, the app warns you and offers to confirm the existing report instead — keeping the map clean and focused.

---

## 🏆 Leaderboard
See who's contributing the most — ranked by karma, with breakdowns of reports submitted, confirmations given, and fixes marked.

## 💀 Hall of Shame
The worst potholes ranked by age, severity, and community frustration. Which neglected pothole is the most infamous?

## 🏛️ Bureaucracy Tracker
Which jurisdictions are fixing potholes quickly, and which are letting them pile up? Tracks fix rates, backlog, and response times by city and county.

---

## 🏘️ Neighborhood Watch Zones
Join or create a watch zone by ZIP code. Each zone has its own discussion board for talking about road conditions, sharing updates, and organizing local action.

## 💬 Threaded Discussions
Posts in watch zones support threaded comments — reply to specific comments to keep conversations organized.

---

## 🐛 Built-in Feedback
A small bug icon in the header lets anyone submit a bug report or feature suggestion directly from the app. Goes straight to the developer.

---

> **The big idea**: Report a pothole in seconds — even hands-free while driving. The app figures out who to contact and sends them the details automatically. The community verifies reports, tracks fixes, and holds local governments accountable.
